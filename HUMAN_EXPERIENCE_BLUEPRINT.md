# Human Experience Blueprint

## Experience Promise

FamilieKompas should feel like a calm human guide that helps a parent find the next step when support is unavailable. The product should never feel like a machine performing therapy, a clinical triage form, or a corporate support portal.

## Emotional Qualities

- Warm without being sentimental.
- Calm without being distant.
- Practical without being cold.
- Premium without feeling exclusive.
- Clear without overexplaining.
- Supportive without pretending to replace professionals.

## Homepage Experience

The homepage should feel like an invitation to breathe and start, not like a sales page.

It should:

- Lead with the family's need for a next step.
- Make the conversation entry point obvious.
- Explain what FamilieKompas does in human language.
- Keep boundaries visible but not intimidating.
- Avoid feature grids, AI positioning, or government-form language.
- Show that the founder's mission is human, while the product remains practical.

The first viewport should answer:

- "Can I safely explain what is happening?"
- "Will this help me find a next step?"
- "Is this therapy, diagnosis, or official advice?" The answer must be clearly no.

## Onboarding Experience

The first screen should make one promise: "Vertel rustig wat er speelt. We helpen je de volgende stap zien."

The onboarding flow should:

- Explain boundaries in plain Dutch.
- Say that FamilieKompas offers guidance, structure, and referral orientation.
- Avoid leading with disclaimers, technology, or features.
- Ask only what is needed to begin.
- Let the conversation start quickly.

## First Conversation Experience

The first conversation should feel like sitting down with someone who can organize a difficult situation.

Future experience principles:

- Start with listening, not intake.
- Ask one question at a time.
- Reflect back the situation in human language.
- Name uncertainty gently.
- Move from emotion to structure to action.
- Offer a visible path without overwhelming the user.

The preferred rhythm is:

1. Listening Mode: "Wat speelt er op dit moment?"
2. Structure Mode: "Ik haal er even de belangrijkste lijnen uit."
3. Action Mode: "Dit kan een eerste rustige stap zijn."

## Follow-Up Conversation Experience

Follow-up should not feel like a ticket system. It should feel like continuity.

Future follow-up should:

- Remember the last agreed next step only with consent.
- Ask what changed since the previous conversation.
- Avoid making the parent repeat the full story.
- Show progress even when the situation is not solved.
- Offer a smaller next step when energy is low.

## Result Experience

The result page should feel like a handover note from a thoughtful guide.

It should include:

- Korte samenvatting.
- Wat lijkt belangrijk.
- Eerste rustige stap.
- Praktische stappen.
- Wanneer hulp zoeken.
- Nuttige hulplijnen en resources when relevant.

It should avoid:

- Diagnostic labels.
- Treatment claims.
- Long generic paragraphs.
- False certainty.
- Overly formal report language.

## Follow-Up Request Experience

Follow-up should be offered only after value is delivered. The request should feel optional and respectful.

Good framing:

- "Wil je dat we hier later op terugkomen?"
- "Je hoeft geen account te maken om nu geholpen te worden."
- "Contactgegevens vragen we pas als dat nodig is voor opvolging."

## Family Portal Emotional Model

The future Family Portal should feel like a private family support folder, not a dashboard.

It should help families:

- See previous guidance.
- Track gentle next steps.
- Save useful preparation notes.
- Control privacy, export, and deletion.
- Feel continuity without feeling monitored.

## Founder Experience Model

The founder experience should protect attention and increase human impact.

The founder should see:

- Crisis and safety alerts first.
- Mission-priority cases second.
- Follow-up requests with concise summaries.
- Suggested review action, not a wall of raw messages.
- Workload limits and status clarity.

## Partner Experience Model

Partners should experience FamilieKompas as a trust network, not a marketplace race.

Future partner interfaces should support:

- Clear role boundaries.
- Verified profile information.
- Referral readiness.
- Consent-based case summaries.
- Outcome feedback without exposing unnecessary family data.

## School And Municipality Experience Model

Schools and municipalities should experience FamilieKompas as support infrastructure.

They should receive:

- Consent-based collaboration tools.
- Aggregated, anonymized insight where appropriate.
- Local resource visibility.
- Clear safeguarding boundaries.

They should not receive:

- Unconsented family stories.
- Hidden profiling.
- Clinical interpretations.
- Surveillance-style analytics.

## What Should Feel Different After Hermes

After Hermes integration, the experience should feel less scripted and more genuinely responsive.

Hermes should improve:

- Emotional nuance.
- Context continuity.
- Better follow-up questions.
- Less repetition.
- More useful summaries.
- Better handoff notes.

Hermes should not change:

- Safety boundaries.
- Non-clinical positioning.
- Consent requirements.
- Founder oversight for mission-priority cases.
- The rule that FamilieKompas offers guidance, not therapy, diagnosis, legal advice, or medical advice.
