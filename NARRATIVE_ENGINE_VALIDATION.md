# Narrative Engine Validation

## Input

"I am a single mother. My son is 8. School says he has behavioral problems. I am exhausted and don't know what to do anymore."

## Previous Behavior

The system recognized facts, emotions, and patterns, then asked a reflection question. It still often repeated the same known facts.

## New Behavior

The system extracts the story behind the facts:

- surface story: school reports behavior concerns
- emotional burden: exhaustion and carrying too much
- pressure source: school pressure
- underlying need: direction and support
- narrative theme: carrying everything alone
- central question: "Can I help my child without going under myself?"

## Follow-Up Example

If the parent later adds:

"I am scared nobody really helps me and I cannot keep doing this."

The response focuses on the new support/fear layer instead of repeating:

- single mother
- son is 8
- school behavior concerns

## Why This Is Better

- It continues the story instead of restarting.
- It reduces repetition.
- It identifies hidden pressure and the question beneath the question.
- It stays deterministic and bounded.
