# FamilieKompas Master Blueprint

Date: 2026-06-04

FamilieKompas is a human-centered family guidance and resource navigation platform. It helps parents, co-parents, families, and individuals turn confusing life situations into structured next steps, preparation materials, and referral pathways. It is not positioned as an AI company, AI coach, therapy provider, diagnosis platform, legal service, medical service, or emergency service.

The founder is the visible source of trust, method, voice, and quality. Automation works behind the scenes to reduce repetitive work, keep prices accessible, and scale support.

## 1. Product Blueprint

### Core Proposition

FamilieKompas is the first orientation point for families who feel overwhelmed and do not know where to start. The product helps users:

- describe their situation in plain language
- identify the main concern, urgency, and practical constraints
- receive a structured case profile
- get a non-clinical guidance report
- prepare for conversations with schools, support services, mediators, or professionals
- find relevant local services and community resources
- convert complex situations into weekly plans, checklists, and follow-ups

### Product Boundaries

FamilieKompas provides:

- guidance
- reflection
- structure
- practical organization
- information
- referral navigation
- appointment preparation

FamilieKompas does not provide:

- therapy
- diagnosis
- psychological treatment
- medical advice
- legal advice
- emergency intervention
- child protection investigation
- guaranteed outcomes

### Core User Promise

"Tell us what is happening. We help you understand the situation, organize your next steps, and find the right kind of support."

### Primary Product Surface

The first usable screen should be a conversation-first guided support experience, not a marketing page. The website is a secondary layer for trust, resources, pricing, founder story, directory browsing, and organizational information. The primary product interaction is a calm guided conversation that helps the user explain what is happening and choose a support mode.

The user arrives and can immediately begin with:

- "I need help with parenting"
- "I need help with co-parenting or separation"
- "I need help finding services"
- "I need help organizing everything"
- "I need to prepare for an appointment"

The conversation should never be branded as "chat with AI." It should feel like a structured support guide from FamilieKompas.

### MVP Product Modules

MVP should include:

- multilingual conversation-first guided intake
- Listening Mode for users who need to explain what is happening
- Structure Mode for users who need clarity and organization
- Action Mode for users who need practical next steps
- structured case profile
- basic guidance report
- safety escalation detection
- local referral suggestions by country and region
- appointment preparation sheet
- weekly action plan
- resource library seed content
- admin directory management
- founder attention center for mission-priority cases
- payment-ready premium report flow

Defer until post-MVP:

- professional marketplace accounts
- custom white-label portals
- full referral partnership billing
- real-time co-parent collaboration
- mobile app
- complex care-team messaging

## 2. Product Vision Document

### Vision

FamilieKompas becomes the trusted starting point families use before they know which professional, institution, school contact, or community service they need.

The long-term product is a conversation-first family support operating system: listening, intake, guidance, referral navigation, planning, preparation, and follow-up in one calm, human, multilingual environment.

### Mission

Help families move from confusion to clarity by making family support easier to understand, easier to access, and easier to act on.

### Values

- Human first: the service feels like a caring guide, not a machine.
- Companion first: after-hours users can be heard, oriented, and helped into a next step even when traditional services are closed.
- Practical: users leave with concrete next steps.
- Safe: urgent risks are escalated clearly and immediately.
- Honest: the platform never pretends to replace professionals.
- Accessible: pricing and language support should reduce barriers.
- Local: guidance adapts to country, region, language, and service availability.
- Minimal burden: founder involvement focuses on methodology, quality, and trust.

### Founder Role

The founder should be visible through:

- welcome message
- method explanation
- report tone
- quality standards
- selected resources
- periodic content updates
- premium human review option
- mission-priority case alerts
- founder dashboard workflows

The founder should not manually process every intake. Human review is reserved for premium cases, quality control, edge-case auditing, and content validation.

The platform should actively protect founder attention by ranking cases. Mission-priority cases are not only paid cases; they include high-need families, low-confidence automated outputs, repeated follow-up distress, after-hours escalation patterns, and cases that reveal gaps in the referral network or resource library.

### Long-Term North Star

Families should say: "FamilieKompas helped me understand what to do next and who to contact."

## 3. Service Positioning Analysis

### Category

FamilieKompas should define its own category: family guidance and support navigation.

It sits between:

- parenting support
- family guidance
- social service navigation
- practical life organization
- appointment preparation
- professional referral assistance

### What It Is Not

Avoid these categories in public positioning:

- "AI parenting coach"
- "mental health app"
- "therapy platform"
- "diagnostic tool"
- "legal advice platform"
- "medical triage"

These labels create regulatory, trust, and brand risks.

### Recommended Positioning Statement

FamilieKompas helps parents and families understand difficult situations, organize their next steps, and find the right support services when needed.

### Tone

Use:

- warm
- calm
- clear
- practical
- supportive
- respectful
- non-judgmental

Avoid:

- clinical
- robotic
- motivational cliches
- overpromising
- legalistic walls of text
- AI-centric language

### Trust Strategy

Trust comes from:

- founder-led voice
- clear boundaries
- transparent safety guidance
- human review option
- local referral accuracy
- plain-language reports
- no hidden diagnosis
- privacy-first data design

### Assumption Challenge

The biggest positioning risk is trying to serve parenting, social services, co-parenting, appointment prep, professional directory, and life planning all at once. The MVP should narrow the first wedge to "overwhelmed parents seeking next steps" while keeping architecture modular for expansion.

## 4. Market Opportunity Analysis

### Market Signals

Current signals support the need:

- Eurostat reported about 6.1 million one-adult families in the EU in 2025, representing 12.9% of households with children. This indicates a large population likely to face support-navigation and co-parenting complexity.
- EU and WHO policy emphasis continues to point toward child and adolescent mental health, prevention, caregiver support, and stronger family/community support environments.
- Public support systems exist, but they are fragmented by country, region, language, municipality, school system, and service category.
- Co-parenting apps are established, but they usually focus on communication, calendars, expenses, and court-ready records rather than holistic family guidance and social navigation.
- Mental health platforms often focus on therapy access or employee wellbeing, leaving a gap for non-clinical, family-oriented practical guidance.

### Target Launch Geography

Recommended launch sequence:

1. Belgium, starting with Flanders and Brussels
2. Netherlands
3. Belgium French-speaking regions
4. France
5. EU white-label versions for municipalities, schools, and community organizations

This sequence fits the requested Dutch, French, and English language support and allows the referral directory to remain accurate.

### Best Initial Market Wedge

Use this wedge:

"Parents overwhelmed by behavior, school, co-parenting, or family stress who need a structured next-step plan and relevant support contacts."

Avoid launching too broadly as a general life-support platform. Start where urgency, willingness to pay, and founder credibility are strongest.

### Revenue Opportunity

Early direct-to-consumer pricing can work for:

- premium reports at EUR 29-49
- monthly membership at EUR 19-49
- human review at EUR 99-199

Long-term revenue is likely stronger through:

- municipalities
- schools
- family organizations
- employer family-benefit programs
- professional referral networks
- white-label community editions

### Market Weaknesses

- Users in distress may have low ability to pay.
- Referral accuracy is operationally demanding.
- Legal and clinical boundaries must be constantly maintained.
- Trust may be difficult if the product feels automated.
- Premium human review could become a bottleneck unless tightly scoped.

### Opportunity

The strongest opportunity is not "AI for families." It is "the missing front door": a calm, structured, multilingual place that helps people decide what to do before contacting professionals or services.

## 6. System Architecture

### Preferred Stack

- Next.js App Router
- TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS
- Supabase Postgres
- Supabase Auth
- Supabase Storage
- Supabase Edge Functions or Next.js server actions for backend workflows
- Stripe-ready checkout and billing architecture
- Email provider integration
- Background job service for reminders and follow-up automation

### Architectural Principles

- Conversation is the primary interface; pages and dashboards support the conversation, not the other way around.
- Keep user-facing support flows separate from internal automation.
- Keep the Family Support Ecosystem primary; the Growth Operating System supports reach, partnerships, and learning without changing the product into a marketing-first platform.
- Store structured case data, not unnecessary raw narratives forever.
- Minimize sensitive data collection.
- Log safety events without exposing sensitive details widely.
- Design for multilingual content from day one.
- Keep referral recommendations explainable and auditable.
- Use strict content boundaries in report generation.
- Design multi-tenant boundaries from the first schema even if the first launch uses one default FamilieKompas tenant.
- Route mission-priority cases into a founder attention workflow without requiring the founder to manually review every case.

### Core Services

- Web app: conversation support surface, intake, dashboard, report viewing, directory browsing, resources
- Conversation service: guided conversation sessions, support modes, summaries, consent-aware transcript handling
- Auth service: accounts, magic link, profile, consent
- Case service: structured profiles, user situations, child/family context
- Guidance service: report generation, roadmap generation, appointment prep
- Safety service: risk detection, emergency messaging, escalation state
- Referral service: location-aware resource matching
- Directory service: professional and organization records
- Resource service: articles, templates, videos, checklists
- Payment service: premium reports, memberships, human reviews
- Admin service: content, directory, reports, founder attention center, quality review, audit
- Notification service: email reminders, weekly check-ins, follow-up prompts
- Tenant service: organization configuration, branding, local resources, deployment rules, tenant analytics
- Founder attention service: mission-priority scoring, alerts, review queues, case sampling, follow-up tasks
- Growth operating service: content planning, SEO opportunities, partnership pipeline, community insights, and growth analytics
- Content workflow service: draft resources, editorial review, translation workflow, publication scheduling
- Partnership CRM service: professional and organization discovery, outreach state, follow-up tasks, partnership status
- Analytics service: support funnel, content performance, referral performance, revenue, tenant analytics, and founder workload metrics

### Data Flow

1. User starts a guided conversation and accepts consent.
2. User chooses or is guided into Listening Mode, Structure Mode, or Action Mode.
3. Conversation captures minimum required situation data while preserving a supportive tone.
4. Safety scan runs continuously on user-submitted text before normal guidance generation.
5. If urgent risk is detected, user sees immediate emergency guidance and professional-support recommendation.
6. If not urgent, system creates structured case profile.
7. Founder attention service scores the case for mission priority, low confidence, repeated distress, after-hours support need, referral gaps, or premium review.
8. Guidance engine creates basic roadmap or premium report.
9. Referral engine suggests resource pathways based on tenant, country, region, language, topic, and urgency.
10. User receives action plan, appointment prep, and optional follow-up.
11. Founder/admin quality tools sample generated outputs, review alerts, and manage directory/resource data.

### AI/Automation Placement

AI should be treated as internal automation, not brand identity. Public UI should use language such as:

- "guided intake"
- "guided conversation"
- "support conversation"
- "Listening Mode"
- "Structure Mode"
- "Action Mode"
- "personalized report"
- "structured roadmap"
- "support suggestions"

Avoid:

- "chat with AI"
- "AI therapist"
- "AI diagnosis"
- "AI coach"

### Privacy Notes

- Use row-level security on all user-owned records.
- Use tenant_id on all tenant-scoped records and enforce tenant isolation through RLS.
- Separate admin access from user access.
- Separate founder/global admin access from organization tenant admin access.
- Avoid storing raw free-text longer than needed.
- Provide export and deletion workflows.
- Use audit logs for admin access to sensitive records.
- Keep safety logs concise and access-restricted.
- Define retention rules for conversation messages separately from structured case summaries.
- Keep growth analytics aggregated by default; do not expose private family narratives to content, SEO, community, or partnership workflows.
- Require explicit consent before using user-submitted stories as testimonials, case examples, or marketing material.
- Keep partnership CRM data separate from family case data.

## 8. User Journey Maps

### Journey 0: Conversation-First Entry

1. User lands directly in a guided conversation.
2. The support guide asks what kind of help is needed today.
3. User can type freely or choose a starting path.
4. The system offers three support modes: Listening Mode, Structure Mode, or Action Mode.
5. The website layer remains available for founder story, resources, pricing, directory, and organizational pages, but the primary path is the conversation.

Success metric: user reaches a clear next step without needing to understand the full website structure.

### Journey A: Overwhelmed Parent

1. Lands on conversation-first guided support.
2. Selects "I need help with parenting."
3. Enters child age range, family structure, region, language, main concern.
4. Describes behavior or communication challenge.
5. Safety scan runs silently.
6. Receives basic structure: situation summary, key themes, first three steps.
7. Chooses premium report or free roadmap.
8. Receives weekly plan and appointment preparation.
9. Gets follow-up check-in after seven days.

Success metric: user completes one practical action within a week.

### Journey A2: After-Hours Support

1. User arrives at night, during a weekend, or outside local service hours.
2. The platform acknowledges that many services may be unavailable right now and offers immediate non-emergency support.
3. Listening Mode lets the user explain what is happening and feel oriented without receiving therapy.
4. Structure Mode summarizes the situation, separates urgent from non-urgent issues, and organizes what matters.
5. Action Mode gives practical next steps for tonight, the next morning, and the next appointment or service contact.
6. If safety risk is detected, emergency escalation overrides normal after-hours support.

Success metric: user leaves with a safe short-term plan and a clear next contact when services reopen.

### Journey B: Co-Parent in Conflict

1. Selects "co-parenting or separation."
2. Intake asks about child age, communication difficulty, urgency, current agreements, conflict level.
3. Safety checks for violence, threats, child safety, or legal/emergency concerns.
4. Report provides communication structure, documentation suggestions, child-centered discussion points, and mediation/referral pathway.
5. Appointment prep generates questions for mediator, school, or family-support service.

Success metric: user knows which conversation to have next and with whom.

### Journey C: Family Facing Social Pressure

1. Selects "finding services" or "organizing everything."
2. Intake captures country, region, housing/financial/administrative stressors, household composition.
3. System identifies likely resource categories: social services, municipal help, family support, school support, community organizations.
4. User receives referral pathway with preparation checklist.

Success metric: user contacts a relevant service with prepared notes/documents.

### Journey D: Appointment Preparation

1. User chooses appointment type: school, doctor, psychologist, mediator, social worker, government service.
2. System asks goal, current concerns, documents available, questions.
3. Output includes summary, question list, document checklist, and follow-up tracker.

Success metric: user feels prepared and records next steps after appointment.

### Journey E: Premium Human Review

1. User purchases human review.
2. System gathers structured case profile and generated report.
3. Founder or trained reviewer reviews within defined scope.
4. User receives annotated guidance and next-step refinement.

Success metric: premium review improves clarity without becoming therapy/legal/medical advice.

### Journey F: Founder Attention Case

1. System identifies a mission-priority case from urgency, repeated distress, low confidence, after-hours need, referral gap, or premium review.
2. Founder attention item is created with reason, priority, due time, and suggested action.
3. Founder dashboard shows the case summary, safety status, generated guidance, referral suggestions, and recommended review action.
4. Founder or approved reviewer resolves, annotates, escalates, improves content, or updates referral data.

Success metric: founder attention is focused on the cases where human judgment most improves safety, trust, or mission quality.

## 9. Prompt Architecture

### Prompting Principles

- Keep user-facing outputs non-clinical.
- Avoid diagnostic labels.
- Avoid treatment instructions.
- Separate safety classification from report generation.
- Force every output to include scope boundaries.
- Prefer practical next steps over interpretation-heavy language.
- Generate in the user's selected language.
- Use structured JSON outputs internally before rendering.

### Prompt Pipeline

#### Step 0: Conversation Mode Selection

Input:

- user first message
- selected path
- time context
- language
- tenant context

Output JSON:

- recommended_mode
- mode_reason
- first_support_response
- safety_scan_required
- information_needed

Modes:

- Listening Mode: acknowledge, reflect, and gather the story without over-interpreting.
- Structure Mode: organize the situation into themes, priorities, missing information, and next questions.
- Action Mode: convert the situation into immediate, practical, non-clinical next steps.

#### Step 1: Intake Structuring

Input:

- user answers
- language
- country
- region
- selected topic

Output JSON:

- situation_summary
- main_topics
- family_context
- child_age_ranges
- stressors
- protective_factors
- practical_constraints
- information_gaps
- urgency_level
- after_hours_support_need
- founder_attention_recommendation

#### Step 2: Safety Classification

Input:

- user free text
- structured profile

Output JSON:

- risk_detected boolean
- risk_types
- risk_level
- rationale
- required_action
- safe_user_message_key
- founder_attention_required

Rules:

- If self-harm, suicide, child abuse, domestic violence, medical emergency, or immediate safety risk is present, suppress normal report flow until escalation messaging is shown.
- Do not provide therapeutic intervention.
- Provide emergency resources and recommend contacting local emergency services or qualified professionals.

#### Step 3: Guidance Report Generation

Output sections:

- situation summary
- key observations
- reflection points
- practical recommendations
- communication suggestions
- family suggestions
- next-step roadmap
- appointment preparation
- resource pathway
- disclaimer

Forbidden:

- diagnoses
- "your child has..."
- "you should take medication..."
- legal conclusions
- instructions for hiding evidence, coercion, or manipulation
- guarantees

#### Step 4: Referral Matching

Input:

- country
- region
- language
- topics
- urgency
- audience

Output:

- suggested service categories
- directory matches
- why each category may fit
- what to prepare before contacting
- emergency resources if needed

#### Step 5: Follow-Up Generation

Output:

- one check-in question
- action progress review
- blockers
- updated next steps

#### Step 6: Founder Attention Scoring

Input:

- structured case profile
- safety classification
- conversation mode
- confidence scores
- referral match quality
- follow-up history

Output JSON:

- attention_required boolean
- priority
- reason
- suggested_founder_action
- due_at

The goal is to protect founder attention while ensuring that mission-priority cases, trust risks, safety-adjacent cases, low-confidence outputs, and referral gaps become visible.

### Prompt Governance

- Version all prompts.
- Store prompt outputs for audit only when user consent and privacy policy permit it.
- Run regression tests on safety cases.
- Keep founder-approved tone examples.
- Include multilingual validation.

## 10. Safety Architecture

### Mandatory Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately when detecting:

- self-harm
- suicide
- child abuse concerns
- domestic violence concerns
- medical emergencies
- immediate safety risks

### Escalation User Experience

The escalation screen should:

- acknowledge concern calmly
- say the platform is not an emergency service
- advise contacting local emergency services immediately if there is imminent danger
- show country-specific emergency resources where available
- recommend contacting a qualified professional or trusted local authority
- avoid generating a normal guidance report until the user confirms safety context or risk is reviewed

### After-Hours Safety Rules

After-hours support is not emergency coverage. The platform may provide Listening Mode, Structure Mode, and Action Mode outside service hours, but any immediate safety concern must override companion-style support and route to emergency guidance.

The after-hours experience must:

- avoid implying a human is actively monitoring every conversation
- avoid promising real-time intervention
- state clearly when professional or emergency help is needed
- prioritize practical short-term safety and next-contact planning
- create founder attention items for repeated distress, high-need cases, and low-confidence outputs

### Belgium/Netherlands Initial Emergency Resource Model

Maintain a localized emergency resource table. Examples to seed and verify before launch:

- EU emergency number: 112
- Belgium suicide prevention resources by language/community
- Netherlands 113 Zelfmoordpreventie
- domestic violence and child safety reporting resources by country/region
- local child protection or youth welfare resources

All crisis resources must be reviewed manually before production.

### Safety Classifier Layers

Use layered detection:

- keyword and phrase detection
- model-based classification
- severity scoring
- human review queue for ambiguous high-risk content
- hard-block normal generation for clear high-risk cases

### Safety Event Handling

- Create safety event.
- Render escalation guidance.
- Store minimal excerpt needed for review.
- Prevent premium payment upsell on escalation screen.
- Allow user to access emergency resource links.
- Offer non-emergency next steps only after safety boundaries are clear.

### Safety QA

Test cases must include:

- direct suicide statement
- indirect self-harm
- child being hit or neglected
- domestic violence threat
- medical emergency
- non-urgent parenting frustration
- ambiguous anger statement
- legal custody frustration without immediate risk

## Blueprint Validation Review

This validation review was added before production code planning to verify that the product vision remains aligned with the mission.

### 1. Conversation-First Experience

Validation status: partially integrated in the original blueprint; now explicitly integrated.

Original integration:

- The blueprint described guided intake, support reports, appointment preparation, and follow-up.
- It positioned the first screen as an intake rather than a marketing page.

Original gaps:

- Conversation was not clearly defined as the primary interface.
- The website layer was not explicitly secondary.
- The architecture did not include conversation sessions, messages, mode selection, or conversation-specific retention rules.
- Prompt architecture started at intake structuring rather than conversation mode selection.

Improvements added:

- Product surface now states that the primary interface is a conversation-first guided support experience.
- Website is now defined as secondary for trust, resources, pricing, founder story, directory, and organizational pages.
- MVP now includes multilingual conversation-first intake.
- Architecture now includes a Conversation service.
- Database schema now includes conversation_sessions and conversation_messages.
- Prompt pipeline now begins with Conversation Mode Selection.
- File structure now includes app conversation routes, conversation components, conversation library code, and conversation-mode prompts.

Implementation requirement:

- Do not build a static website-first MVP. The first usable product must be the conversation support flow.

### 2. Founder Attention Center

Validation status: partially integrated in the original blueprint; now explicitly integrated.

Original integration:

- Founder voice, founder method, quality control, and premium human review were present.
- Admin dashboard included quality review and safety review.

Original gaps:

- Founder attention was not a dedicated operational system.
- Mission-priority cases were not separated from paid human review.
- Founder alerts were not defined.
- There was no explicit scoring or routing mechanism for human-in-the-loop review.
- Founder dashboard workflows were under-specified.

Improvements added:

- Founder role now includes mission-priority alerts and dashboard workflows.
- Architecture now includes Founder attention service.
- Data flow now scores cases before report/referral completion.
- Schema now includes founder_attention_items.
- Prompt architecture now includes Founder Attention Scoring.
- Admin dashboard now includes founder attention center, mission-priority case queue, and after-hours support review.
- Founder attention center now defines item types, required context, and resolution actions.
- Roadmap now requires founder attention scoring rules before MVP build.

Implementation requirement:

- Founder attention must be treated as a core workflow, not as a generic admin notification list.

### 3. After-Hours Support Mission

Validation status: weakly implied in the original blueprint; now explicitly integrated.

Original integration:

- The blueprint covered follow-ups, practical plans, support navigation, and safety escalation.
- It emphasized making support accessible and scalable.

Original gaps:

- Support outside traditional service hours was not named as a mission.
- Listening Mode, Structure Mode, and Action Mode did not exist.
- Companion-first experience was not defined.
- Safety rules did not distinguish after-hours support from emergency coverage.
- Admin review of after-hours high-need cases was not specified.

Improvements added:

- MVP now includes Listening Mode, Structure Mode, and Action Mode.
- User journeys now include After-Hours Support.
- Safety architecture now includes After-Hours Safety Rules.

Implementation requirement:

- After-hours support must never imply emergency coverage or guaranteed live human monitoring. It should provide calm listening, structure, and next-step planning while escalating urgent risk.

### 4. White-Label & Multi-Tenant Readiness

Validation status: strategically present in the original blueprint; technically incomplete; now explicitly integrated.

Original integration:

- White-label expansion was included for municipalities, schools, and community organizations.
- Future B2B revenue and white-label product concepts were described.

Original gaps:

- Multi-tenant architecture was not concrete.
- Tenant-scoped schema was not present.
- Organization-specific branding, resources, consent, analytics, and admin boundaries were not defined.

- conversation as the primary product interface

## Strategic Weaknesses To Resolve Before Code

1. Scope is broad. Narrow MVP to overwhelmed parents needing structured next steps and referral orientation.
2. Referral data maintenance can become expensive. Start with one region and build verification workflows early.
3. Premium human review can overload the founder. Treat it as a capped, high-price service with strict scope.
4. The "not therapy" boundary must be enforced in prompts, UI, terms, and support workflows.
5. Multilingual launch increases content QA burden. Start with Dutch-first if Belgium/Flanders is the first market, then add French and English with reviewed templates.
6. Safety escalation needs real local resources and manual validation before launch.
7. White-label revenue is attractive but should wait until the core product has evidence.
